Step-by-Step How-Tos

Plan & Launch a VTOL Flight

From planning to safe transition and cruise, this procedure describes a standard commercial operation.

  1. Go to Planner → New Flight.
  2. Define the Mission Area and place waypoints.
  3. Enable Geofence and set RTH.
  4. Verify RTK/GPS lock, battery SOC, and actuator status.
  5. Arm, perform a Hover Check, then Transition to cruise.
  6. On arrival, select Auto-Land or manual VTOL landing.
Tip Wind > 12 m/s: extend hover check and consider lower transition speed.

Swap a Payload & Re-Calibrate

Safely change cameras or LiDAR and update center-of-gravity (CG) for stable transitions.

  1. Open Payloads → New Profile.
  2. Enter mass and mounting position to compute CG offset.
  3. Attach the payload; secure all connectors.
  4. Run Gimbal Cal and Sensor Cal.
  5. Save as a preset and test a short hover with Stability Monitor.
Safety Never exceed rated payload mass or CG limits shown in the profile.

Troubleshooting (Commercial Operations)

Aircraft won’t transition to forward flight expand collapse
  1. Check wind component; reduce gross weight or increase climb to cleaner air.
  2. Verify tilt actuator status and error codes in Flight Monitor.
  3. Confirm CG within limits (Payloads → active profile).
  4. Retry transition at recommended airspeed and altitude.
RTK/GPS won’t lock expand collapse
  1. Move away from structures; check sky view > 60%.
  2. Verify base-station link and correction stream.
  3. Fallback to GPS only and expand geofence margins.
Battery/ESC over-temperature alert expand collapse
  1. Reduce power demand; climb slowly; avoid prolonged hover.
  2. Land and inspect motor ducts and heat sinks for blockage.
  3. Check cell balance and cycle count; schedule maintenance if repeated.
Excessive vibration in hover expand collapse
  1. Run prop balancer; inspect blades for chips and pitch mismatch.
  2. Check motor mount torque and arm stiffness; recalibrate IMU.
  3. Verify payload mount isolation and gimbal damping.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is SkyLift VTOL for?

Industrial and commercial operators: inspection, mapping, logistics, public safety.

What are typical wind limits?

Plan operations below 12 m/s steady wind for transitions, and follow your aircraft limitations.

How often is maintenance required?

Follow flight-hour and cycle-based inspections for motors, ESCs, actuators, and batteries.

How do I manage payload CG?

Use Payload Profiles to track mass and mount position; the console computes CG offset and flags out-of-range setups.

Where do I export flight data?

Open Logs → Export (CSV/JSON) and attach to support cases as needed.

Is there a dark mode?

Not included by default, but can be added by applying a dark theme.
